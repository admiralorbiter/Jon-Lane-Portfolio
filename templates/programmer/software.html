From the moment we got a family computer, a generous gift from my uncle when I was in 6th grade, my fascination with programming took root. I devoted three years to mastering Visual Basic 6, crafting innovative applications for Yahoo and AOL chat services. By the time I entered 9th grade, I was eager to expand my skills and immediately enrolled in my high school's advanced programming class, where I honed my abilities in C++. My passion and skill didn't go unnoticed; I clinched several awards in programming competitions and even developed custom applications for users.

In 2010, I embarked on my professional journey as a Java developer, creating sophisticated software solutions for an auto repair shop. This role marked the beginning of a deep engagement with Spring Boot and a variety of other Java frameworks that significantly expanded my technical repertoire.

Since 2016, I've shifted my focus towards nurturing the next generation of programmers by teaching high school students and developing web-based applications. My programming adventures continue as I extensively use Python, which has become my preferred backend language, proving essential in both educational settings and professional development projects.
