PREP-KC Websites

Over the last few years in my position at PREP-KC, I have developed several websites:

  • prepkc.org
  • Math Relays Website
  • Data Science Website
  • Pathways to Tech Website

I began my journey in web development in 2001, when I was in 6th grade, thanks to a family computer purchased by my uncle. From 2001 to 2008, I devoted my free time to mastering HTML 4.0 and CSS3, creating websites on a Gateway computer using dial-up internet.

These skills were honed through countless interactions on message boards and chat rooms, where I sought and shared advice. By my senior year of high school, I had started to earn money through contract work, developing simple applications for volunteer management among other tasks.

Since 2016, my professional role has heavily involved web development. I've taught multiple courses and led projects, deepening my expertise in HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap by developing from official documentation.

Recently, I've embraced a streamlined stack for frontend development using HTML/CSS, Bootstrap, and HTMX, with SQLite for databases (switching to PostgreSQL when necessary). For backend development, I prefer Flask or Django.
